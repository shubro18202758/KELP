## Business Description

Allcargo Gati is a leading logistics and supply chain management company in India, specializing in express distribution, warehousing, and transportation solutions. The business model focuses on providing tailored logistics services across various sectors, including e-commerce, automotive, and chemicals, leveraging advanced technology for real-time tracking and efficient operations. With over 30 years of experience, Allcargo Gati has delivered more than 344 million packages and saved over 25,310 tons of CO‚ÇÇ emissions, showcasing its commitment to sustainability and operational excellence.

## SWOT

### Strengths
- Allcargo Gati's FY25 net profit reached Rs 10 crore, a significant rise from Rs 4 crore .
- Q4 2025 revenue increased 9% to Rs 385 crore, with net profit zooming to Rs 15 crore .
- Gross margins improved by 5%, and revenue grew by 2% year-on-year in FY25 .
- Allcargo Gati covers 99% of India's districts, offering unparalleled reach across Asia .
- EBITDA margin grew remarkably to 5.5% from 1.9% in Q3 of the previous year .

### Weaknesses
- Net profit declined 8.19% to Rs 13.34 crore in FY25, compared to Rs 14.53 crore .
- Net sales decreased from Rs 1,863.19 crore in March 2019 to Rs 1,509.94 crore in March 2025 .
- Allcargo Gati reported a 96.6% decline in net profit at Rs 1 crore in Q3 .
- PBILDT margins declined from 4.07% in FY23 to 3.07% in FY24 due to higher costs .
- Surface express contributed 91.10% of TOI in FY24, reflecting a concentrated revenue profile .

### Opportunities
- Express logistics sector is set to grow at ~14% CAGR from FY23 to FY28 .
- Gati aims to capitalize on growth by establishing mega hubs and enhancing efficiency .
- Government's Gati Shakti plan integrates 39 ministries for infrastructure development .
- Logistics costs in India have dropped from 13-14% of GDP to 7.8-8.9% .
- India's rank in Logistics Performance Index jumped from 54th in 2014 to 38th in 2023 .

### Threats
- Challenges like funding gaps and land acquisition delays remain for Gati Shakti .
- Allcargo Gati announced a 10.2% average General Price Increase (GPI) from January 2025 .
- The GPI aims to counterbalance rising operational costs driven by inflation .
- India's GDP growth rate was 6.5% in 2024-2025, indicating potential economic fluctuations .
- The logistics sector faces challenges in achieving complete convergence across ministries .
